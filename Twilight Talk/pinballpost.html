Twilight Zone Pinball Machine

August 12, 2018 by Douglas Hawkes

In April of 1993, popular game developer and publisher, Midway, released the Twilight Zone based off the TV series and was designed by Pat Lawlor. I'm told that this is one of the most complex pinball machines of all time and that there are several ways to actually complete the object of the game which is to unlock the 14 panels in the center playfield to unlock "Lost in the Zone". I won't go over all the details of how to win and rack up points but I will do a short review of this hidden gem.

Presentation

First off I want to say that I have never played, nor have I seen this pinball machine in real life. I'm basing this off of various videos I have watched on youtube. I would love to play this game but I don't have an emulator so I can't.

From the looks of the front, it honestly just looks like it has a lot going on, but it looks like once you get started everything seems to sort out and run pretty smoothly, and you can't help but notice all of the nostalgic elements behind the glass that make it more exciting. The design of Twilight Zone is brilliant, it looks like designers give you several chances for big points and rewards but if you are bad enough to actually miss these big shots then your chances of the ball going down is very high so it's high risk high reward which I very much enjoy. I love how Rod Serling is always sitting above the door panel just reminding you that this machine must have come straight out of the twilight zone seeing as how hard this game looks. When everything starts alot of lights and flickering and flashing making it pretty cool and I also enjoy the music that comes on too. It sounds a little fuzzy but still makes you enjoy and appreciate that you're actually playing a Twilight Zone pinball game thats fun.

I think one of the best parts about this game is that it has so many icons from the actual series, which include:

  • The door from the intro
  • The mannequin doll from the intro
  • The clock from the intro
  • A picture of the guy from the episode, "Time Enough At Last"
  • The hitchhiker from the episode
  • The slot machine from "The Fever"
  • The camera from "A Most Unusual Camera"
  • The small spacemen from "The Invaders"
  • The radio from "Static"
  • The piano from "A Piano in the House
  • Talky Tina from "Living Doll"
  • The robot from "Uncle Simon"

With all these iconic figures within the machine, it's extrememly hard to expect anything less than a superb game. This presentation definitely receives an excellent rating overall.

Gameplay

When you start the game the clock turns on too and automatically knows what time it is which I think is pretty cool. The goal of the game is to light all 14 door panels in the front starting "Lost in the Zone" mode. To get these panels lit, you have to wait for random things in the game to light up and then shoot it with the ball. For instance you can shoot the player piano when lit and you'll be awarded one panel that is flashing. Once the panel is lit, you must make a shot correlating with what the panel says. If you unlock a panel that says "The Spiral" then you must shoot the ball into the spiral to be awarded big points and this is also a timed mode.

This game looks incredibly fun and the music from the jackpot makes it oh so satisfying. I alos like how you don't have to play to get the all the panels unlocled and that you can play just to rack up millions of points. I will say this looks extrememly hard and if you're not persistent, it may not be the pinball game for you. The "pop bumpers" are THE worst. I literally watched someone try to hit a ramp and got stuck in the pop bumpers 3 times in a row to lose. Even making the slot machine, the piano, and the mini playfield shot are hard and thats basically how to have to get all the panels lit.

When "Lost in The Zone" starts by shooting the camera when the doorknob is available, the machine goes ballistic and everything lights up and it looks fantastic and they even show the intro scene on the user interface when it starts. You get all the balls including the power ball which you have to put back in the gumball machine which looks like one of the most difficult things to do. Once you do finally beat Lost in the Zone you get an insane amount of points and you beat the game and it allows to start over.

Conclusion

Twilight Zone pinball machine is a great investment if you have the funds. I would asbolutely purchase this game if it wasn't over 5,000 dollars on ebay. There are even mods for this game that they also sell online like an LED rocket ship, LED flying doll, and LED talky tina which are over 50 dollars a piece. I definitely reccomend trying this pinball machine if you can, I have not been so lucky enough to find one around where I live to enjoy so I will still be on the lookout for them.

Overall the game just looks like some real fun with some real challenges as well which makes the player hungry for more and consistently coming back to triumph Lost in the Zone or even reach this rare mode.
